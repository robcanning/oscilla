Programme Note

1:10,560 (6 inches to the Mile) unfolds as a meditation on journeys‚Äîboth personal and collective. Inspired by the evocative language of maps, the piece reflects on how we chart, explore, and interpret the world around us. Maps have long been tools of discovery, capturing landscapes and sparking imagination, yet they also serve as instruments of power, imposing boundaries and reshaping perceptions. This duality‚Äîa source of wonder and a reminder of historical and ongoing traumas‚Äîinforms the piece's emotional and conceptual landscape. The score itself acts as a metaphorical map, with dynamic elements inviting performers to chart their own paths. Symbols in the graphic score directly reference the visual language of Irish Ordnance Survey maps, their contours echoing themes of exploration and control. The music resists easy categorization, offering moments of spontaneity, reflection, and raw intensity. While rooted in historical cartography, the piece also turns its gaze to the present. Digital cartographies extend beyond GPS systems, reflecting how our interactions are continuously mapped‚Äîfrom browsing habits to personality profiling. These processes shape our perceptions, often targeting us with precision while keeping us within curated echo chambers. In this way, mapping becomes yet another instrument of control, reinforcing the dominance of global tech corporations as the new colonial powers of a digital age. Against this backdrop, 1:10,560 encourages us to pause and reflect on the act of mapping itself‚Äîboth as a metaphor and as a means of understanding the landscapes within and around us. The composition's score is a networked digital artefact, blending traditional and graphic notation with media elements such as animation and audio-visual cues. It creates a dynamic, ever-shifting terrain for performers to navigate. Performers are guided but not constrained, navigating a rich and interpretive environment that mirrors the complexities of exploration and choice.
